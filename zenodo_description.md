# Zenodo Record Description

## Title

**Mathematical Analysis of the Cone Surface: Curvature, Surface Area, Volume, and Differential-Geometric Properties**

---

## Authors

**Dogan Balban**

---

## Description

This work presents a complete mathematical and differential-geometric analysis of the right circular cone defined (in cylindrical coordinates) by

$$z = -\frac{3}{4} + \frac{\pi}{2}\,r,$$

originally given in the more complex form

$$z = -\frac{1}{12}\cdot 3^{2} + 6\cdot\sqrt{\frac{x^{2}}{(12/\pi)^{2}}+\frac{y^{2}}{(12/\pi)^{2}}}.$$

### Contents

The paper covers:

- **Algebraic simplification** — step-by-step reduction to cylindrical form
- **Geometric characterisation** — identification as a right circular cone with apex $(0, 0, -3/4)$, slope $m = \pi/2$
- **Cone angles** — cone half-angle $\alpha = \arctan(2/\pi) \approx 32.48°$ (angle between axis and generator); complementary elevation angle $\beta = \arctan(\pi/2) \approx 57.52°$; full apex angle $2\alpha \approx 64.96°$
- **Partial derivatives** (first and second order) and gradient magnitude $|\nabla f| = \pi/2$ (constant)
- **Parametrization** in cylindrical coordinates with tangent vectors and cross product
- **Lateral surface area** — $A = \pi\sqrt{1+\pi^2/4}\cdot(r_2^2-r_1^2)$
- **Volume** — $V = (4/3\pi)\left[(h_2+3/4)^3-(h_1+3/4)^3\right]$
- **Gaussian curvature** $K = 0$ (developable surface)
- **Mean curvature** $H = \pi/(4r\sqrt{1+\pi^2/4})$
- **Geodesics** and development of the cone into a planar sector
